About

I am an illustrator and software engineer at NASA.

I am based in Night City, aka the Bay Area pre-Cyberpunk.

This site was previously what scavengers-repo was for, but I’ve since moved towards git pages to get rid of the overhead.

By day, I am usually working with code snippets and network traffic. Obviously I can’t go into a lot specifics of what we do, but it’s pretty empowering to compare hacking scenes with real life

My digital self is more into the artsy, [gaming, comics, anime] scene. I was a closeted geek way back in the early 2000s. I might miss some of those times but I sure like having a job that’s able to fund my hobbies and no pressure of school and all the drama of being a teenager. Now I get to game, paint, and pull espresso shots while waiting for Cyberpunkian future to take place in real life.

You can check me out on ArtStation and Instagram. I spend so much time staring at screens, that sketching stuff out on pencil and paper is a relief. That being said, tablets, styluses, and art software have come quite a long way, all the stuff you see on here is stuff I've made.

My favorite thing to do is character design; I started out by wanting to breathe some life into the DND campaigns my friends would run. I do a sketch every Saturday now, usually it's Bloodborne stuff, but I like mixing them with my coding notes. My goal is to build a library of technical notes, retro tech, and alchemical grimoires laden with illustrations and diagrams detailing cryptids next to network diagrams. Now that would have gotten my stupid younger self interested in engineering.
