About

Welcome to my site. This was previously what scavengers-repo.com was for, but updating it became a huge overhead so looks like I'll be in git pages moving forward.

About me... I'm just a tech worker in the Bay, grew up as a closeted geek and pretty unaware of it in the early 2000s until I went off to college in the 2010s to "explore" the wider world. I picked up software engineering back when it was mostly iPhone app dev and no limits, opened up the InfoSec skill tree path, and everything's been peachy. I can't really go into the specifics of what I do but I guess you can check out my LinkedIn if you're actually into that professional landscape.

My digital self is more into the artsy, [gaming, comics, anime] scene. I didn't grow up playing games beyond Banjo Tooie and Spyro on my older sister's friend's GameCube while they snuck off to play Mech Warrior, at least until I got brave enough to buy an XBox in college.

It's been mostly PC games in between my dayjob and nightjob. I don't get a lot of time to play, so my gAm3R Sk1LlZ are pretty much relegated to casual tier, but I try to at least have one game I play consistently, even if it's just a week. My kid-side tells me that having an ongoing game improves the quality of my work so maybe, just maybe there is some element of truth to that.

I definitely did not grow up with comics since I was never a huge fan of the bigger DC and Marvel superhero comic genre, but I'm a really big fan of Dark Horse and Titan Comics. They usually are the ones to publish the video game comics that I follow like Bloodborne and Horizon Zero Dawn. It seems so archaic in a time of online shopping and pirating, but I have a soft spot for local comic book/game shops. I may not have grown up with vintage tech and retrogaming (I'm a young millenial/old gen z'er so I barely remember the 2000s let alone the 90s), but they are a relic from an earlier time. Whatever bells and whistles we have now, are built off of the things that era/subculture started and perpetuated

What else about me, I do art both IRL and in a digital medium via Procreate. You can check me out on ArtStation and Instagram. I spend so much time staring at screens, that sketching stuff out on pencil and paper is a relief. That being said, tablets, styluses, and art software have come quite a long way, all the stuff you see on here is stuff I've made.

My favorite thing to do is character design; I started out by wanting to breathe some life into the DND campaigns my friends would run. I do a sketch every Saturday now, usually it's Bloodborne stuff, but I like mixing them with my coding notes. My goal is to build a library of technical notes, retro tech, and alchemical grimoires laden with illustrations and diagrams detailing cryptids next to network diagrams. Now that would have gotten my stupid younger self interested in engineering.
